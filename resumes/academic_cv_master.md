## Sandip Dayani

PhD Candidate, Mechanical and Mechatronics Engineering  
University of Waterloo  
Research area: crystal plasticity, micromechanics, damage mechanics, and failure prediction in advanced high-strength steels.

## Research Statement Summary

My research connects microstructural deformation mechanisms to macroscopic failure in advanced high-strength steels. I develop crystal-plasticity damage models that account for crystallographic slip, void nucleation and growth, phase interactions, strain-rate sensitivity, texture evolution, and temperature rise during plastic deformation. The broader goal is to build validated constitutive models that improve failure prediction in automotive and safety-critical structures.
