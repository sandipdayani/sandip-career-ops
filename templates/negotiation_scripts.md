# Negotiation Scripts

## When Asked For Salary Expectations

For US industry roles:

> Based on the role scope, PhD-level modeling requirements, and my background in GM-sponsored AHSS failure prediction, I am targeting roles from 125,000 USD and above. I am open to discussing the full package depending on location, relocation support, bonus, benefits, and growth path.

For Canada industry roles:

> For Canada-based industry roles, I am targeting 100,000 CAD and above, depending on the full package, location, technical scope, and long-term growth path.

For academic roles:

> For academic or postdoctoral roles, I would evaluate compensation together with research fit, publication opportunity, funding stability, immigration path, and long-term career value.

## When An Offer Is Below Target

Thank you for the offer. I am excited about the technical fit and the opportunity to contribute to [team/project]. Based on the role scope and my PhD-level experience in crystal-plasticity damage modeling, finite element implementation, and GM-sponsored AHSS failure prediction, I was expecting compensation closer to [target number].

Is there flexibility to bring the base salary closer to that range, or to improve the total package through signing bonus, relocation support, performance bonus, or earlier review?

## When You Want Time To Decide

Thank you. I appreciate the offer and I am taking it seriously. I would like to review the full package, role scope, relocation details, and long-term growth path before giving a final answer. Can I get until [date] to respond?

## Trade Secret Boundary During Negotiation

Use this if they press for details:

> I can discuss the technical direction, modeling strategy, validation logic, and engineering value at a high level. I cannot share confidential GM details such as equations, calibrated parameters, source code, internal datasets, or implementation specifics.
