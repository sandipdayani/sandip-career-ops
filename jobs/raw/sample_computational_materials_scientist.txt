Title: Computational Materials Scientist - Advanced Steels
Company: Example Automotive R&D Center
Location: United States or Canada
Salary: 130000 USD

We are seeking a computational materials scientist to develop physics-based models
for deformation, damage, and fracture in advanced steels used in automotive
lightweighting and crash-critical components. The role will combine crystal
plasticity, finite element implementation, constitutive model calibration, and
experimental validation using stress-strain data, DIC, EBSD, and thermal
measurements.

Preferred qualifications include a PhD in mechanical engineering, materials
science, computational mechanics, or a related field. Experience with CPFEM,
damage mechanics, ductile fracture, strain-rate effects, temperature rise during
plastic deformation, and microstructure-sensitive modeling is strongly preferred.
Experience with Python, Fortran, Abaqus, LS-DYNA, or other finite element tools
is valuable.

The successful candidate will work with automotive engineering teams to reduce
trial-and-error testing, improve validated material cards, and support predictive
simulation workflows for forming and crashworthiness.
