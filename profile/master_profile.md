# Master Profile: Sandip Dayani

## Core Identity

Computational materials researcher and PhD candidate in Mechanical and Mechatronics Engineering at the University of Waterloo. Specializes in crystal plasticity, micromechanics, damage mechanics, strain-rate and temperature-dependent deformation, and failure prediction in advanced high-strength steels.

## Current Research Positioning

I develop physics-based micromechanical models that connect grain-scale deformation and damage mechanisms to engineering-scale stress-strain softening, localization, and fracture. My PhD work focuses on crystal-plasticity damage modeling for automotive advanced high-strength steels, including HSLA, DP, and Q&P steels.

## Highest-Value Career Signal

Developed a CPDM framework for GM-sponsored AHSS failure prediction, later retained as a GM trade secret. The framework integrates rate-dependent crystal plasticity, damage evolution, thermo-mechanical coupling, and experimental validation using EBSD, DIC, and IR thermography.
