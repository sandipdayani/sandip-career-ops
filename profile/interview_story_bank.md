# Interview Story Bank

## Story 1: GM-Sponsored CPDM Work

**Use for:** crystal plasticity, automotive R&D, confidentiality, failure prediction, model validation.

**Situation:** GM needed physics-based understanding of AHSS failure behavior for steels relevant to automotive lightweighting and crash-critical design.

**Task:** Build a crystal-plasticity damage modeling workflow that connected microstructural mechanisms to macroscopic stress-strain softening and failure.

**Action:** Developed a CPDM framework for HSLA, DP, and Q&P steels; connected slip, damage evolution, strain-rate effects, and temperature response with EBSD/DIC/IR-informed validation.

**Result:** The framework was retained as GM trade-secret IP. In interviews, describe the engineering value and validation loop, not confidential equations, parameters, or internal implementation details.

## Story 2: International Journal Of Plasticity Paper

**Use for:** publication quality, model novelty, validation, independent research capability.

**Situation:** Failure prediction in advanced steels required linking microstructure-sensitive deformation, damage, temperature rise, and strain-rate response.

**Task:** Produce a publishable model and validation workflow with enough rigor for a high-level mechanics/materials journal.

**Action:** Combined crystal plasticity, damage mechanics, finite element implementation, and experimental comparison against stress-strain and thermal response data.

**Result:** Published first-author work in the International Journal of Plasticity and built a strong foundation for industry-facing AHSS failure prediction.

## Story 3: Experimental Validation Loop

**Use for:** roles requiring model calibration, test-data interpretation, EBSD, DIC, IR thermography.

**Situation:** A purely numerical model would not be credible without direct comparison to microstructure and deformation measurements.

**Task:** Use experimental data to constrain and validate the model.

**Action:** Used EBSD for microstructure/texture context, DIC for strain fields, IR thermography for temperature rise, and stress-strain data for macroscopic response.

**Result:** Built a stronger link between local mechanisms and engineering-scale material response.

## Story 4: Implementation Under Constraints

**Use for:** Fortran, Python, FEA, code implementation, debugging, numerical methods.

**Situation:** Research models are only useful if they can be implemented robustly enough for simulation workflows.

**Task:** Turn constitutive and damage modeling ideas into working computational routines.

**Action:** Worked with Fortran/Python-supported finite element workflows, model calibration, post-processing, and validation checks.

**Result:** Developed implementation experience that transfers to UMAT/VUMAT, LS-DYNA, Abaqus, and internal material model workflows.

## Story 5: Cross-Domain Modeling Range

**Use for:** broader industry roles beyond steel, especially aerospace, nuclear, energy, simulation software.

**Situation:** Before the PhD, projects covered thermal systems, composites, additive manufacturing, and CFD.

**Task:** Build simulation capability across multiple physics domains.

**Action:** Worked on PCM borehole heat exchanger modeling, FRP micromechanics, diesel combustion CFD, and thermal/flow simulation projects at research institutes.

**Result:** Built a broader computational engineering base while keeping the current career identity centered on materials modeling and failure prediction.
