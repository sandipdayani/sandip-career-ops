# Proof Points

Use these as evidence when tailoring resumes, cover letters, recruiter messages, and interview answers.

## 1. GM Trade-Secret CPDM Work

**Claim:** Developed a CPDM framework for GM-sponsored AHSS failure prediction, later retained as a GM trade secret.

**Evidence to mention:** HSLA, DP, and Q&P steels; rate-dependent crystal plasticity; damage evolution; thermo-mechanical coupling; EBSD/DIC/IR validation.

**Do not mention:** confidential equations, parameters, GM database details, source code, or unpublished internal conclusions.

**Resume bullet:**

Developed a CPDM framework for GM-sponsored AHSS failure prediction, later retained as a GM trade secret; integrated rate-dependent crystal plasticity, damage evolution, thermo-mechanical coupling, and EBSD/DIC/IR-based validation.

## 2. Published IJP Paper

**Claim:** First-author peer-reviewed publication in a leading mechanics journal.

**Evidence:** International Journal of Plasticity, vol. 186, 104255, 2025.

**Resume bullet:**

Published a first-author International Journal of Plasticity paper on thermodynamically consistent crystal-plasticity damage modeling for HSLA steels across strain rates.

## 3. Experimental + Modeling Loop

**Claim:** Can connect real experiments to validated constitutive models.

**Evidence:** EBSD for microstructure and texture; DIC for strain fields; IR thermography for temperature rise; tensile testing across strain rates.

**Resume bullet:**

Integrated EBSD, DIC, IR thermography, and strain-rate-dependent tensile testing to calibrate and validate microstructure-sensitive failure models.

## 4. Constitutive Model Implementation

**Claim:** Not just theory; implemented material models.

**Evidence:** Fortran, material-point implementation, user-defined material subroutine workflows, LS-DYNA/Abaqus/ANSYS context.

**Resume bullet:**

Implemented crystal-plasticity and damage-model routines in Fortran-based workflows, enabling prediction of softening, temperature rise, and failure trends under high-rate deformation.

## 5. Industry Relevance

**Claim:** Work is directly relevant to automotive lightweighting and crashworthiness.

**Evidence:** GM-sponsored work; AHSS systems; forming and failure prediction; crash-critical material behavior.

**Resume bullet:**

Supported automotive lightweighting and crashworthiness research by developing validated AHSS failure models aimed at reducing trial-and-error material testing.

## 6. Broader Simulation Background

**Claim:** Experience extends beyond steel to thermal systems, composites, and CFD.

**Evidence:** Fraunhofer UMSICHT BHE/PCM modeling; Fraunhofer IWS nozzle flow; DLR automated fibre placement; TU Chemnitz FRP RVE; bachelor combustion CFD.

**Resume bullet:**

Built multiphysics simulation experience across steel failure, geothermal heat exchangers, additive manufacturing flow, composite RVEs, thermal aerospace manufacturing, and combustion CFD.

## 7. Teaching and Communication

**Claim:** Can explain mechanics clearly.

**Evidence:** TA for ME559 and ME219 at University of Waterloo.

**Resume bullet:**

Led tutorials, graded assignments, and supported student projects in mechanics courses, strengthening technical communication and mentoring skills.
